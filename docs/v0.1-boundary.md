# v0.1 Scope and Boundary

This document defines the scope, non-goals, adoption boundary, review expectations,
non-canon posture, required packet pieces, and adjacent repository links for v0.1
of the Agent Instruction Format.

## Status

Candidate namespace. Exploratory. Not a standard.

Nothing in v0.1 is adopted guidance. v0.1 only proposes a minimal, reviewable
shape for an agent-facing instruction packet so that later review can accept,
reject, or replace it.

## Scope

v0.1 covers the smallest useful unit of agent instruction material: a single,
self-describing **instruction packet** that:

- Declares its own identity, version, and scope.
- Declares its authority and contract (instruction type, hierarchy level,
  override rules, inheritance policy).
- Declares what it expects from a reviewer or executor (receipt requirements).

v0.1 does **not** define:

- A runtime, agent loop, or execution semantics.
- A tool-calling protocol.
- A model-specific prompt encoding.
- A registry, catalog, or discovery mechanism.
- A governance process for adoption (that is a later roadmap item).

## Non-goals

- Not a universal standard for agent instructions.
- Not a replacement for `agents.md`, system prompts, or existing prompt-template
  libraries.
- Not a claim of ownership over the field, the vocabulary, or the term
  "instruction".
- Not a canonization of any private, internal, or vendor-specific concept.
- Not a channel for private, internal, or secret operational content.

## Adoption boundary

v0.1 is **candidate only**. Adoption is explicitly out of scope for this version.

Any artifact in this repo marked `packetStatus: candidate` is a proposal, not
guidance. Adoption criteria, review gates, and a promoted `adopted` status are
deferred to a later roadmap milestone. Consumers of v0.1 artifacts must not
treat them as normative.

## Review expectations

A v0.1 packet is expected to be:

- **Reviewable** — small enough for a human to read in one pass.
- **Auditable** — carries its own identity, version, and provenance fields.
- **Testable** — has a JSON Schema (`schemas/agent-instruction-format-v0.1.json`)
  and at least one valid and one invalid fixture.
- **Boundary-preserving** — contains no private operational content.

Reviewers should reject any v0.1 packet that:

- Omits a required field.
- Claims `adopted` status (not permitted in v0.1).
- Embeds private, internal, or secret material.
- Claims ownership over terminology it does not own.

## Non-canon posture

This repository does not canonize concepts. Specifically:

- HUMMBL, BaseN, and Ownward concepts are **not** canonized here unless
  explicitly marked, audited, and adopted through a future governance path.
- Vendor-specific prompt formats (OpenAI, Anthropic, Google, etc.) are
  referenced as prior art, not as normative references.
- The vocabulary collected in `docs/prior-art.md` is descriptive, not
  prescriptive.

## Required packet pieces

A v0.1 instruction packet must contain, at minimum:

1. `schemaVersion` — the schema version this packet conforms to (`v0.1`).
2. `packetStatus` — one of `candidate` (v0.1 only permits `candidate`).
3. `instructionManifest` — identity block:
   - `id` — stable packet identifier.
   - `title` — human-readable title.
   - `version` — packet version (semver-ish string).
   - `scope` — short statement of what the packet covers.
4. `authority` — who/what issued the packet and its non-canon posture.
5. `instructionContract` — the executable contract:
   - `instructionType` — e.g. `system`, `user`, `task`, `convention`.
   - `hierarchyLevel` — integer rank in an instruction hierarchy (lower = stronger).
   - `content` — the instruction content itself (string or structured object).
   - `overrideRules` — how this packet interacts with conflicting instructions.
   - `inheritancePolicy` — whether/how child contexts inherit this instruction.
6. `receiptRequirements` — what a reviewer or executor must confirm before
   acting on the packet.

See `schemas/agent-instruction-format-v0.1.json` for the normative shape and
`examples/instruction-packet-v0.1.example.json` for a worked example.
